About Me



I'm Nuel Anak Deli. I mostly work in software programming, but I've found exciting adventures in radio astronomy, music, and everything in between.

My tech journey started in an unexpected place. It was 2018, and I was a Social Science graduate working as a graphic designer. My evenings after work were often spent playing around with Python code. I found myself yearning for something more hands-on—something that would allow me to create. That’s when I dove into coding, teaching myself programming languages like Python and JavaScript, along with frameworks like Django. I've been doing software programming since late 2019.

My interest in astronomy sparked when I was 11 years old. It all began in the school library, where I discovered a book about Galileo Galilei and his scientific work, especially regarding the telescope. Since then, I’ve spent countless evenings after school watching astronomy documentaries. In adulthood, I’ve continued to fulfill my curiosity about the universe and science by reading the works of Michael Faraday, Isaac Newton, Albert Einstein, Nikola Tesla, Stephen Hawking, and Michio Kaku.

At 19, inspired by Led Zeppelin, I decided to pick up the guitar. Since then, my interest in music has grown significantly, and I’ve begun to write my own songs. I mainly write for myself, and those tracks have yet to see the light of day (just kidding).
